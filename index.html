sonder

n. the realization that each passer-by is living a life as vivid and complex as your own.

You will meet almost no one.

Of the thousands who pass within reach of you — a face behind a lit window, a name read once and never again, the blur at the edge of someone else's photograph — each holds a world as wide as the one behind your own eyes. Loves you will never hear named. A grief shown to no one. An ordinary evening you will never witness.

Here, every point is one of them. Linger on one and a sliver of that life surfaces — then the current carries them on, and you will not find them again. Not because they are gone, but because there were always this many, and we only ever stop for a few.

A small place to practice looking.
